  • Welcome to the Course!

    Dear Students,                                                                                                                                                                               

    Welcome to CCHU 9076, the Common Core Course entitled "The Journalist; Comics, Movies, Fiction and Fact."

    First, this is not a journalism course.  You will not be learning the skills of reporting, like writing news leads, shooting video, interviewing sources or preparing TV packages. We will be talking a lot about journalists and journalism, but this course does not teach you to be a journalist.

    Also, this is not a history course, although we will be talking some about recent, and not-so-recent, history, like the Vietnam War, the Khmer Rouge era in Cambodia and the Watergate scandal in the U.S. This is not a film class or a literature course, but will be reviewing some classic films and works of fiction, like Scoop by Evelyn Waugh and The Quiet American by Graham Greene.  And we will talk a lot about comic book characters.

    So what is this course about?

    In this course, we will be looking at all the ways the journalist has been depicted in popular culture -- starting with comic books, looking at movies, and reading classic and popular fiction.  We'll then look at the work of real journalists, and maybe hear directly from some in class, to help you better understand how well those fictional portrayals match up to the real thing. There will be a lot of outside class work; you will be watching assigned movies and reading from novels and nonfiction books about reporters and their craft.  Rather than feeling like you are doing boring homework out of boring textbooks, we hope you will find these assignments exciting, thought-provoking and fun.

    After taking this course, you will not be able to leap tall buildings in a single bound, outrun a speeding bullet or stop a fast-moving freight train with your bare hands (unless you could do those things before). The course will, however, help you heighten your "Spidey senses" and hone your own superpowers, whatever those may be.  The next time you see a journalist character in a movie, you will be able to tell your friends; "That was ridiculous -- no real journalist would ever do that!"  And when they ask how you would know, you can reply; "Because I took CCHU 9076" and you can showcase your unmatched intelligence. You will become far more popular and sought after at parties, and the friends in your social media circle will expand by the thousands.

    This is a time when media is under attack from many quarters, including those who want to brand all reporting they disagree with as "fake news."  We hope this course will also leave you with a better understanding and appreciation of the tough job real journalists do, often in dangerous and difficult conditions, in trying to dig for the truth, separate fact from fiction and avoid the ethical pitfalls that reporters regularly face. 

    Some of you, after taking this course, might decide to become journalists yourselves, and you will be welcomed to the ranks like a new member joining the Avengers. Others we hope may just become more deliberate and discriminating news consumers. And all of you will go out into the world as mighty defenders of truth, accuracy, objectivity and the journalistic way.

    Students Assemble!

      • IMPORTANT: Updates and clarifications about your final presentation

        For the final group presentation, you can do your presentation ZOOM LIVE or PRE-record. We understand that PRE-recording presentations can be time consuming, so we hope this flexibility will make it easier. Either way, please have good internet connection while delivering your presentation.  

        For the ONE DRIVE submission link, please submit these items 24 hours before your scheduled presentation 

        1. the project itself (the comic book, the podcast, the movie, etc.)
        2. the PRE-recorded presentation, if available (one mp4 file). You do not need to submit this if your group is presenting live 

        For your presentation, you are expected to summarize your project, talk about your project, and answer questions about your project. You do not necessarily have to make a powerpoint for your presentation. It is up to your team how to best deliver your project to the audience. Please keep your entire presentation under 20 minutes. 

        If your project is a short story or comic book, your team is recommended to share *excerpts of your work* to the class. If your project is a movie or podcast, your team is recommended to share *the entire movie or podcast* to the class.

        **Correction: If your project is a movie and you choose to PRE-record your presentation, DO NOT PRE-record yourself screen sharing the movie. That is not necessary.**

        Let me know if you have questions!
